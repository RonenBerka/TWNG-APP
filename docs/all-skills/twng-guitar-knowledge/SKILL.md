## Major Guitar Brands

### American Brands

**Fender** (1946-)
- Founder: Leo Fender
- Key Models: Stratocaster (1954), Telecaster (1950), Jazzmaster (1958), Jaguar (1962)
- Serial Number Format: Varies by era (see serial number section)
- Acquired: CBS (1965), sold to investors (1985)
- Subsidiaries: Squier (budget line), Jackson, Charvel, Gretsch (2002)

**Gibson** (1902-)
- Founder: Orville Gibson
- Key Models: Les Paul (1952), SG (1961), ES-335 (1958), Flying V (1958)
- Serial Number Format: Varies by era
- Subsidiaries: Epiphone (budget line), Kramer, Steinberger
- Bankruptcy: 2018, emerged same year

**Martin** (1833-)
- Founder: C.F. Martin Sr.
- Focus: Acoustic guitars
- Key Models: D-28, D-18, OM-28, 000-28
- Known for: Dreadnought shape innovation

**PRS (Paul Reed Smith)** (1985-)
- Founder: Paul Reed Smith
- Key Models: Custom 24, McCarty, Silver Sky
- Known for: High-end craftsmanship, bird inlays

**Rickenbacker** (1931-)
- Key Models: 330, 360, 4001 (bass)
- Known for: Beatles association, jangle sound

### Japanese Brands

**Ibanez** (1957-)
- Parent: Hoshino Gakki
- Key Models: RG, JEM, S series, Artcore
- Known for: Shred guitars, extended range

**ESP** (1975-)
- Subsidiaries: LTD (mid-range), Edwards (Japan)
- Key Models: Eclipse, Horizon, M-II
- Known for: Metal guitars

**Yamaha** (1887-, guitars since 1940s)
- Key Models: Pacifica, SG, FG series (acoustic)
- Known for: Quality at all price points

### British Brands

**Gretsch** (1883-)
- Key Models: White Falcon, Country Gentleman, Duo Jet
- Known for: Rockabilly, hollow bodies
- Now owned by Fender

## Guitar Body Styles

| Style | Description | Typical Use |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Solid Body** | No sound chamber | Rock, metal, blues |
| **Hollow Body** | Full hollow chamber | Jazz, rockabilly |
| **Semi-Hollow** | Center block with hollow wings | Blues, rock, jazz |
| **Acoustic** | Hollow with sound hole | Folk, country, singer-songwriter |
| **Classical** | Nylon strings, wider neck | Classical, flamenco |

### Common Body Shapes

- **Stratocaster**: Double cutaway, contoured body
- **Telecaster**: Single cutaway, slab body
- **Les Paul**: Single cutaway, carved top
- **SG**: Double cutaway, thin body
- **Superstrat**: Strat-based with modern features
- **Dreadnought**: Large acoustic body
- **Jumbo**: Larger acoustic body
- **Parlor**: Small acoustic body

### Wood Types (Tonewoods)

**Body Woods**:
- **Alder**: Balanced, common on Fender
- **Ash**: Bright, swamp ash popular
- **Mahogany**: Warm, common on Gibson
- **Basswood**: Neutral, popular for shred guitars
- **Maple**: Bright, often used as top

**Neck Woods**:
- **Maple**: Bright, snappy
- **Mahogany**: Warm

**Fretboard Woods**:
- **Maple**: Bright, smooth
- **Rosewood**: Warm, oily
- **Ebony**: Bright, fast, dense
- **Pau Ferro**: Rosewood alternative

## Serial Number Decoding

### Fender (USA)

**Pre-1976**: Various formats
**1976-present**: First letter = decade, second = year

| Prefix | Years |
|--------|-------|
| S | 1970s |
| E | 1980s |
| N | 1990s |
| Z | 2000s |
| US | 2010s |

Example: Z5xxxxx = 2005

### Gibson

**1977-present**: First digit = year (or first two)
- 70xxxxx = 1970s
- 80xxxxx = 1980s
- 8 digits starting with year

## Condition Grading

| Grade | Description | Value Impact |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Mint** | Unplayed, tags attached | 100%+ |
| **Near Mint** | Barely played, no wear | 95-100% |
| **Excellent** | Light play wear | 85-95% |
| **Very Good** | Normal wear, fully functional | 70-85% |
| **Good** | Obvious wear, works fine | 50-70% |
| **Fair** | Heavy wear, may need work | 30-50% |
| **Poor** | Project guitar | <30% |

### Wear Indicators
- Fret wear (divots, flat spots)
- Finish checking (lacquer cracks)
- Hardware corrosion
- Electronics function
- Neck relief/playability
- Originality (parts replacement)

## Vintage Market Basics

### What Makes a Guitar Valuable

1. **Brand & Model**: Iconic names command premiums
2. **Year**: Pre-CBS Fender, 50s Gibson = top value
3. **Condition**: Originality is key for vintage
4. **Provenance**: Celebrity ownership, studio use
5. **Rarity**: Limited editions, special runs
6. **Documentation**: Papers, receipts increase value

### Key Vintage Years

**Fender**:
- Pre-CBS (before 1965): Most valuable
- CBS era (1965-1984): Less valuable
- Post-CBS (1985+): Modern vintage

**Gibson**:
- 1950s Les Pauls: Holy grail
- 1960s: Pre-Norlin valuable
- Norlin era (1969-1986): Generally less valuable
