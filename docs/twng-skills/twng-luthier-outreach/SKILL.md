## Understanding Luthiers

### Who They Are
- **Luthiers**: Build guitars from scratch
- **Guitar Technicians**: Repair and setup existing instruments
- **Authorized Service Centers**: Brand-affiliated repair shops
- **Independent Repair Shops**: General instrument repair

### Their Pain Points
1. **Visibility**: Hard to get discovered by new customers
2. **Portfolio**: No easy way to showcase work history
3. **Credibility**: Difficult to prove expertise
4. **Documentation**: Time-consuming to track repairs for customers
5. **Communication**: Customer follow-up is manual

### What They Value
- Reputation and word-of-mouth
- Quality over quantity
- Respect for the craft
- Long-term customer relationships
- Fair compensation for expertise

## Value Proposition for Luthiers

### TWNG Offers

1. **Digital Portfolio**
   - Every guitar you work on becomes part of your portfolio
   - Customers document your work automatically
   - Searchable history of your repairs

2. **Verification Badge**
   - Verified Luthier status on TWNG
   - Authentication stamps on guitars you've worked on
   - Professional credibility marker

3. **Customer Discovery**
   - Appear in luthier directory
   - Searchable by location and specialty
   - Reviews from TWNG users

4. **Streamlined Documentation**
   - Add repair notes directly to guitar's timeline
   - Photo documentation integrated
   - Digital records for your business

## Outreach Strategy

### Finding Luthiers

**Online Sources**:
- Google Maps: "guitar repair" + city
- Facebook: Guitar repair groups
- Reverb: Listed service providers
- Guild of American Luthiers directory
- Local musician forums

**Offline Sources**:
- Music stores (referrals)
- Recording studios
- Music schools
- Guitar shows and exhibitions

### Prioritization Criteria

| Criteria | High Priority | Medium | Low |
|----------|---------------|--------|-----|
| Reputation | Well-known | Established | New |
| Online Presence | Active | Basic | None |
| Specialty | Vintage/High-end | General | Basic |
| Location | Major city | Secondary | Remote |
| Responsiveness | Quick reply | Slow | No reply |

### Outreach Sequence

1. **Day 0**: Initial email
2. **Day 4**: Follow-up if no response
3. **Day 10**: Final follow-up
4. **Day 14**: Move to "cold" list, try again in 3 months

## Objection Handling

### "I don't have time"
> "I understand. The good news is TWNG doesn't require extra work from you. Your customers document their guitars and tag your work. You just need to create a profile once, and the rest happens automatically."

### "I don't need more exposure"
> "That's great that business is good. TWNG isn't just about finding new customers - it's about documenting your legacy. Every repair you do becomes part of that guitar's permanent history. It's a digital portfolio of your craftsmanship."

### "What does it cost?"
> "TWNG is free for luthiers during our founding member phase. We're building a community, not selling advertising. Down the road, there may be premium features, but basic listing and verification will always be free."

### "I'm not tech-savvy"
> "No problem. Setting up a profile takes about 10 minutes, and we can do it together over a call. After that, everything is automated - you don't need to do anything technical."

## Success Metrics

- Number of luthiers contacted
- Response rate
- Conversion rate (signed up)
- Profile completion rate
- Active luthiers (monthly activity)
- Verifications performed
- Customer reviews received

## Luthier Communication Best Practices

1. **Respect their time** - Keep messages short
2. **Show you know their work** - Reference something specific
3. **Lead with value** - What's in it for them
4. **Be patient** - They're busy with actual work
5. **Follow up** - But don't spam
6. **Speak their language** - Technical credibility matters
