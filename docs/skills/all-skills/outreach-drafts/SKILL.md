## Cold Email Framework

### The AIDA Structure

```
A - Attention: Hook in first line
I - Interest: Relevant problem/opportunity
D - Desire: Your solution/value
A - Action: Clear, low-friction CTA
```

### Cold Email Template (English)

**Subject Lines (Test Multiple):**
- Question about [topic they care about]
- [Mutual connection] suggested I reach out
- Quick question about [their company]
- Idea for [specific challenge]

```
Hi [Name],

[HOOK - One sentence that shows you did research]

I noticed [specific observation about their company/content/activity].

[BRIDGE - Connect to problem you solve]

[Companies like theirs / Their industry] often struggle with [specific problem].

[VALUE - What you offer, briefly]

At [Company], we help [target audience] [achieve result] by [method].

[PROOF - One credibility point]

[Customer name] saw [specific result] after [timeframe].

[CTA - Low friction ask]

Would you be open to a 15-minute call to see if this could help [company name]?

[Sign-off]
[Name]
```

## LinkedIn Outreach

### Connection Request Template

```
Hi [Name],

[Reason for connecting - be specific]

Examples:
- I enjoyed your post about [topic] - especially [specific point].
- We're both in [industry/group] and I'd love to connect.
- [Mutual connection] suggested we connect.
- I'm impressed by [their work/company achievement].

Looking forward to connecting,
[Name]
```

### LinkedIn Message After Connection

```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for connecting!

[Bridge to value]

I noticed [observation]. At [Company], we help [audience] with [problem].

Would you be interested in [specific offer/resource/conversation]?

[Name]
```

### LinkedIn InMail Template

```
Subject: [Personalized hook]

Hi [Name],

[Compliment or observation - shows research]

Quick question: [Problem-focused question]

I ask because [brief context about what you do and why it matters to them].

If [problem] is on your radar, I'd love to share [resource/idea/case study].

Worth a quick chat?

[Name]
```

## Follow-Up Sequences

### Follow-Up Timing

| Follow-Up | Days After | Purpose |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| 1st | 3-4 days | Gentle bump |
| 2nd | 7 days | Add value |
| 3rd | 14 days | Different angle |
| 4th | 30 days | Break-up |

### Follow-Up Templates

**Follow-Up #1 (Bump)**
```
Hi [Name],

Just floating this back to the top of your inbox.

[Original ask]

Worth a quick chat?

[Name]
```

**Follow-Up #2 (Add Value)**
```
Hi [Name],

I came across [article/resource/insight] and thought of [their company].

[Brief summary of why it's relevant]

Still happy to chat about [original topic] when timing works.

[Name]
```

**Follow-Up #3 (Different Angle)**
```
Hi [Name],

Trying a different approach -

What if I shared [specific deliverable] that shows exactly how [solution] could work for [company]?

No commitment, just wanted to show instead of tell.

Interested?

[Name]
```

**Follow-Up #4 (Break-Up)**
```
Hi [Name],

I haven't heard back, so I'll assume the timing isn't right.

I'll stop reaching out, but if [problem you solve] becomes a priority, I'm here.

Wishing [company] continued success.

[Name]
```

## Partnership Outreach

### Partnership Proposal Template

```
Subject: Partnership idea: [Your Company] x [Their Company]

Hi [Name],

I've been following [Their Company]'s work in [area], especially [specific achievement].

I think there's an interesting opportunity for [Your Company] and [Their Company] to work together.

**The Idea:**
[1-2 sentences on partnership concept]

**Why It Works:**
- For you: [Benefit to them]
- For us: [Benefit to you]
- For our customers: [Mutual customer benefit]

**What I'm Proposing:**
[Specific, low-commitment next step]

Would you be open to a 20-minute call to explore this?

[Name]
[Company]
```

### Influencer/Creator Outreach

```
Subject: Love your work on [specific content]

Hi [Name],

I've been following your [content type] on [platform] - especially loved [specific piece].

I'm [Name] from [Company]. We [what you do] for [audience].

I think there could be a great fit for collaboration:

**Idea:** [Specific collaboration concept]

**What We Offer:**
- [Benefit 1: Product/compensation]
- [Benefit 2: Exposure/content]
- [Benefit 3: Creative freedom]

Interested in discussing?

[Name]
```

## Referral Request Templates

### Asking for Introduction

```
Hi [Name],

Hope you're doing well!

I noticed you're connected to [Target Name] at [Company].

I'm reaching out to companies like theirs to discuss [topic]. Would you be comfortable making an introduction?

Happy to send you a blurb to forward if that's easier.

Thanks!
[Name]
```

### Forwardable Blurb

```
Hi [Referrer Name],

Here's a blurb you can forward:

---

Hi [Target Name],

[Referrer] suggested I reach out.

I'm [Name] from [Company]. We help [audience] [achieve result].

Given [Target Company]'s focus on [relevant area], I thought there might be an interesting conversation here.

Would you be open to a brief call?

[Name]

---

Thanks for passing this along!
```

## Response Handling

### When They're Interested

```
Great to hear from you, [Name]!

[Answer any questions they asked]

How does [Day] at [Time] work for a quick call?

Here's my calendar link if easier: [Link]

[Name]
```

### When They Say "Not Now"

```
Totally understand, [Name]. Timing is everything.

Would it be okay if I checked back in [timeframe]?

In the meantime, feel free to reach out if anything changes.

[Name]
```

### When They Say "Not Interested"

```
Appreciate the response, [Name].

I'll remove you from follow-ups. If anything changes, I'm always happy to chat.

Wishing you and [Company] all the best.

[Name]
```

## Outreach Best Practices

### Do's âœ…

- Personalize every message
- Keep it short (under 100 words for cold emails)
- Have one clear CTA
- Follow up (most deals happen after 5+ touches)
- Test subject lines
- Send at optimal times

### Don'ts âŒ

- Don't attach files in cold emails
- Don't use "just checking in"
- Don't write essays
- Don't be pushy
- Don't lie about mutual connections
- Don't spam

### Subject Line Tips

**High performers:**
- Questions
- Numbers/specifics
- Name drops (carefully)
- Pattern interrupts

**Low performers:**
- All caps
- Excessive punctuation
- Clickbait
- "Free" or "Sale"
