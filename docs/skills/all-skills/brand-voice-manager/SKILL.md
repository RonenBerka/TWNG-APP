## Brand Voice Framework

### 1. Voice Attributes Matrix

Define your brand's core voice attributes (choose 3-5):

| Attribute | We Are | We Are Not | Example |
|-----------|--------|------------|---------|
| **Friendly** | Warm, approachable | Overly casual, sloppy | "We're here to help" vs "Hey dude!" |
| **Professional** | Expert, reliable | Cold, corporate | "Our expertise..." vs "Per our policy..." |
| **Innovative** | Forward-thinking | Jargon-heavy | "New approach" vs "Synergize solutions" |
| **Authentic** | Genuine, honest | Fake, salesy | "Here's what we found" vs "AMAZING DEAL!" |
| **Empowering** | Supportive, enabling | Preachy, condescending | "You can do this" vs "You should..." |

### 2. Tone Adaptation Guide

Tone shifts based on context while voice stays consistent:

| Context | Tone Shift | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| **Celebration** | Enthusiastic, warm | "We're thrilled to announce..." |
| **Apology** | Sincere, humble | "We understand this affected you..." |
| **Education** | Clear, patient | "Here's how it works..." |
| **Urgency** | Direct, focused | "Action needed: ..." |
| **Support** | Empathetic, helpful | "We hear you. Let's solve this..." |
| **Sales** | Confident, value-focused | "Transform your [X] with..." |

### 3. Style Guide Components

#### Grammar & Mechanics
| Element | Standard | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Oxford comma | Yes/No | "A, B, and C" or "A, B and C" |
| Contractions | Yes | "We're" not "We are" |
| Numbers | Spell 1-9, digits 10+ | "five items" vs "15 items" |
| Dates | Month DD, YYYY | January 15, 2026 |
| Times | 12-hour with AM/PM | 9:00 AM |
| Currency | Symbol before | â‚ª150, $50 |

#### Capitalization Rules
| Element | Style | Example |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Headlines | Title Case | "How to Build Your Brand" |
| Subheads | Sentence case | "Tips for better writing" |
| Product names | As trademarked | iPhone, LinkedIn |
| Job titles | Lowercase unless before name | "the CEO" vs "CEO John Smith" |

### 4. Terminology Management

#### Word Choice Standards
| Use | Don't Use | Why |
|-----|-----------|-----|
| "Help" | "Assist" | More human |
| "Use" | "Utilize" | Simpler |
| "Buy" | "Purchase" | More direct |
| "Start" | "Commence" | Clearer |
| "Show" | "Demonstrate" | Friendlier |
| "About" | "Approximately" | Shorter |

### 7. Brand Voice Audit Checklist

Before publishing, verify:

- [ ] **Tone matches context** - Appropriate for the situation
- [ ] **Voice attributes present** - Can identify our core traits
- [ ] **No banned words** - Avoided forbidden terminology
- [ ] **Consistent terminology** - Used approved terms
- [ ] **Appropriate formality** - Matches channel
- [ ] **Active voice** - Primarily active, not passive
- [ ] **Clear and concise** - No unnecessary words
- [ ] **On-brand CTAs** - Actions align with voice
- [ ] **Inclusive language** - Welcoming to all

### 8. Voice Training Examples

#### Rewrite Exercise
**Off-brand**: "We are pleased to inform you that your order has been processed and will be dispatched shortly."

**On-brand** (Friendly Professional):
"Great news! Your order is on its way. You'll receive tracking details soon."

#### A/B Voice Test
**Version A** (More formal):
"Our team of experts is available to assist with your inquiries."

**Version B** (More friendly):
"Got questions? We're here to help."
