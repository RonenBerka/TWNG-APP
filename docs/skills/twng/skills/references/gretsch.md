## üìÖ Key Eras

### Golden Era (1954-1965)

| Model | Year |
|-------|------|
| Duo Jet | 1953 |
| 6120 | 1955 |
| White Falcon | 1955 |
| Country Gentleman | 1957 |

**Era identifiers:**
- "Fred Gretsch" logo
- DeArmond pickups (early)
- Filtertron (1957+)
- Horseshoe headstock

### Baldwin Era (1967-1985)

- Quality decline
- Production issues
- Some good examples exist

### Revival Era (1985-2002)

- Fred Gretsch Jr. revives brand
- Japan production begins
- Quality returns

### Fender Era (2003-Present)

- Fender acquires brand
- Expanded lines
- Consistent quality

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Identification Mistakes

### 1. Japan vs USA

**Check:**
- Serial prefix (JT/JD = Japan)
- Price point
- "Made in Japan" label

### 2. Electromatic vs Professional

**Electromatic tells:**
- "Electromatic" on headstock
- Broad'Tron pickups
- Chrome hardware (usually)
- Lower binding quality

### 3. Vintage Value

**Pre-Baldwin (1967) commands premium:**
- Check serial number era
- "Brooklyn" or early "Booneville"
- Fred Gretsch Mfg. Co. label
