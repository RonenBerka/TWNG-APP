# Forgotten American Brands (1950s-1970s)

> **Coverage:** Harmony, Supro, Kay, Kalamazoo, Silvertone, Danelectro, National, Airline
> **Era:** Golden Age budget instruments, now collectible
> **Source Reference:** vintageguitarandbass.com

---

## Overview

These brands were the "budget" instruments of the 1950s-1970s â€” sold through department stores, catalogs, and music shops. Today, many are highly collectible due to:
- Unique construction (Res-o-glas, Masonite, unusual woods)
- Famous players who used them
- Distinctive aesthetics
- Relative affordability vs. vintage Fender/Gibson

## Harmony

### Company History
- **Founded:** 1892, Chicago
- **Peak:** 1950s-1960s (largest guitar manufacturer in the US)
- **End:** 1975 (bankruptcy)
- **Production:** Millions of instruments
- **Sold at:** Sears, department stores, music shops

### Key Models

| Model | Type | Era | Notable Features |
|-------|------|-----|------------------|
| **Rocket** | Electric | 1950s-1960s | Space-age design, often 3 pickups |
| **Stratotone** | Electric | 1953-1970s | Single cutaway, H-series |
| **H-22** | Acoustic | 1950s-1960s | Budget dreadnought |
| **H-75** | Electric | 1960s | Multi-pickup hollowbody |
| **Sovereign** | Acoustic | 1960s-1970s | Jumbo acoustic, good value |
| **Bobkat** | Electric | 1960s | Short scale, student guitar |

### Serial Numbers
- **Pre-1973:** No consistent system
- **Dating:** Often use pot codes or construction details
- **Stamps:** May have date stamps inside body

### Identification Tips
- Look for "Harmony" on headstock (sometimes "H" logo only)
- Many were sold under other names (Silvertone for Sears)
- Check for Chicago address on labels
- Gold/bronze foil pickups are common

### Value Range
| Condition | Typical Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Player grade | $200-500 |
| Excellent | $500-1,200 |
| Mint vintage | $1,000-3,000+ |

## Supro

### Company History
- **Parent:** National/Valco (Chicago)
- **Era:** 1935-1968
- **Revival:** 2013+ (new company)
- **Famous for:** Res-o-glas fiberglass bodies, Thunderbolt amps

### Key Models

| Model | Type | Era | Notable Features |
|-------|------|-----|------------------|
| **Dual Tone** | Electric | 1954-1968 | Used by Jimi Hendrix |
| **Belmont** | Electric | 1950s-1960s | Res-o-glas body |
| **Sahara** | Electric | 1960s | Used by Jimmy Page |
| **Coronado** | Electric | 1960s | Higher-end model |
| **Ozark** | Acoustic | 1960s | Resonator guitar |

### Construction
- **Res-o-glas:** Fiberglass body with wood core
- **Tone:** Distinctive, warm, slightly lo-fi
- **Weight:** Light (fiberglass)
- **Durability:** Resistant to temperature changes

### Famous Users
- **Jimi Hendrix:** Early recordings with Supro
- **Jimmy Page:** "Whole Lotta Love" tone
- **David Bowie:** Various recordings

### Serial Numbers
- Inside control cavity or on neck pocket
- Often 3-4 digits
- Cross-reference with catalog years

### Value Range
| Condition | Typical Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Player grade | $800-1,500 |
| Excellent | $1,500-3,500 |
| Rare models | $3,000-8,000+ |

## Kalamazoo

### Company History
- **Parent:** Gibson (budget brand)
- **Era:** 1930s-1970
- **Made in:** Kalamazoo, Michigan (Gibson's factory)
- **Purpose:** Entry-level Gibson-quality instruments

### Key Models

| Model | Type | Era | Notable Features |
|-------|------|-----|------------------|
| **KG-1** | Electric | 1960s | Student SG-style |
| **KG-2** | Electric | 1960s | SG-style with vibrato |
| **KG-11/12** | Acoustic | 1930s-1960s | Small body, great blues guitar |
| **Model A** | Mandolin | Various | F-style mandolin |

### Relationship to Gibson
- Same factory, same workers
- Lower-tier materials
- Simpler construction
- Often same quality control

### Identification Tips
- "Kalamazoo" on headstock
- Gibson-style construction
- No "Gibson" anywhere
- Simpler binding/inlays than Gibson

### Serial Numbers
- Often none or stamped numbers
- Use construction features for dating
- Check for FON (Factory Order Numbers)

### Value Range
| Condition | Typical Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Player grade | $200-500 |
| Excellent | $500-1,200 |
| 1930s flat-top | $1,500-4,000+ |

## Danelectro

### Company History
- **Founded:** 1947, New Jersey
- **Era:** 1947-1969, revival 1998+
- **Famous for:** Masonite/vinyl bodies, lipstick pickups
- **Sold as:** Danelectro, Silvertone (Sears), Airline (Montgomery Ward)

### Key Models

| Model | Type | Era | Notable Features |
|-------|------|-----|------------------|
| **U2** | Electric | 1950s-1960s | Double cutaway |
| **Longhorn** | Bass | 1958-1968 | Distinctive shape |
| **Convertible** | Electric | 1960s | Hollow body |
| **3021** | Electric | 1960s | Jimmy Page guitar |

### Construction
- **Body:** Masonite top/back, poplar frame
- **Pickups:** "Lipstick tube" (actual lipstick tubes)
- **Tone:** Unique, chimey, lo-fi
- **Weight:** Very light

### Famous Users
- **Jimmy Page:** "Kashmir" solo
- **Jimi Hendrix:** Various recordings
- **Beck:** Modern user

### Identification Tips
- "Coke bottle" headstock
- Lipstick tube pickups
- Concentric "stacked" knobs
- Vinyl tape binding

### Serial Numbers
- Inside control cavity
- Often stamped on neck pocket
- No consistent system

### Value Range
| Condition | Typical Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Player grade | $400-800 |
| Excellent vintage | $800-2,000 |
| Longhorn bass | $1,500-4,000+ |

## Dating Without Serial Numbers

Since most of these brands lacked consistent serial numbers, use:

1. **Pot Codes**
   - Format: 137-XXYY (137 = CTS, XX = year, YY = week)
   - Example: 137-6520 = 1965, week 20

2. **Catalog Research**
   - vintageguitarandbass.com for original catalogs
   - Match features to catalog year

3. **Construction Changes**
   - Pickup designs changed over years
   - Tuner styles evolved
   - Logo/headstock shapes changed

4. **Hardware Dating**
   - Kluson tuners have date codes
   - Bridge styles indicate eras
