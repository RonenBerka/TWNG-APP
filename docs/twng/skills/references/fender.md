- 3 single coils (standard)

| Model | Pickups | Key Features | Era |

**Telecaster Visual ID:**
- Single cutaway slab body
- 2 single coils (standard)
- 3-way selector
- Fixed bridge with 3 brass saddles (vintage) or 6 saddles (modern)
- String-through body

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Identification Mistakes

### 1. Squier vs Fender
**Always Check:**
- Headstock logo (Squier says "Squier by Fender" or just "Squier")
- Neck plate country stamp
- Serial number prefix

### 2. Mexico vs USA
**Differences:**
- Serial prefix (MX vs US)
- Neck plate stamp
- Quality of hardware
- Fret edges (USA cleaner)

### 3. Japan vs Mexico
**Both are good quality!**
- Japan: JD/MIJ/CIJ serial
- Often better than similar-era Mexican

### 4. Vintage vs Reissue
**Reissue tells:**
- Modern serial number
- Modern poly finish (unless relic)
- Perfect frets and binding
- Modern pots/electronics

### 5. Relic vs Real Wear
**Relic indicators:**
- Too perfect wear patterns
- Consistent aging throughout
- Modern underneath (electronics, pots)
- Custom Shop paperwork

- [ ] Serial number matches era
- [ ] Pot codes match (within 1-2 years)
- [ ] Neck date stamp present
- [ ] Correct logo style

- [ ] CZ or CN serial prefix
