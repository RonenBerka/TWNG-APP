### Pickup Specifications Guide

**Key Pickup Specs to Document:**

| Spec | What It Means | Impact on Tone |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| **DC Resistance (k惟)** | Coil wire resistance | Higher = hotter output |
| **Inductance (H)** | Magnetic field strength | Affects resonance peak |
| **Magnet Type** | AlNiCo II/III/V, Ceramic | AlNiCo=warmer, Ceramic=brighter |
| **Output (mV)** | Signal strength | Higher = more drive |
| **Resonant Peak (kHz)** | Frequency emphasis | Higher=brighter, Lower=warmer |

**Magnet Types Quick Reference:**
- **AlNiCo II**: Warm, vintage, lower output (PAF-style)
- **AlNiCo III**: Weakest, very warm, clean
- **AlNiCo V**: Brighter, tighter, higher output
- **AlNiCo VIII**: Very strong, tight bass
- **Ceramic**: Bright, aggressive, high output

**Note**: Resonance curves (frequency response graphs) provide better tonal comparison than DC resistance alone. DC resistance is easy to measure but doesn't tell the whole story.

##  Serial Number Decoding

### General Tips
- Serial numbers can appear on: headstock, neck plate, heel, inside body
- Same number format may have been used across years
- Always cross-reference with other dating methods
- Serial numbers are ONE piece of the puzzle - verify with other evidence

###  Additional Authentication Methods
Beyond serial numbers, use these to verify date/authenticity:

| Method | Location | What It Reveals |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **Potentiometer Codes** | Inside electronics cavity | Week/year of pot manufacture |
| **Neck Date Stamps** | Neck heel or pocket | Date neck was made |
| **Capacitor Codes** | Electronics cavity | Era of components |
| **Pickup Stamps** | Bottom of pickups | Production date |
| **Body Stamps** | Inside body cavity | Production info |
| **Tuner Stamps** | Underside of tuners | Manufacturer/date |

**Pot Code Reading (CTS format):**
- Format: 137-XXYY (137 = CTS)
- XX = Year (2 digits), YY = Week
- Example: 137-8934 = 34th week of 1989

**Japanese Emperor Dating Codes:**
Some Japanese guitars (Alvarez-Yairi, others) use traditional Japanese calendar:
- **Showa Era** (1970-1988): Codes 45-63 = years 1970-1988
- **Heisei Era** (1989-2000): Codes 1-12 = years 1989-2000
- Example: First two digits "52" = 1977 (Showa 52)

### Gibson Serial Numbers

**Modern (1977-present) - 8 digits:**
Format: YDDDYPPP
- Y = Year (first digit)
- DDD = Day of year (001-365)
- Y = Year (second digit if needed)
- PPP = Plant/Production number

**Examples:**
- 82765501 = 1985, 276th day
- 90234002 = 1999, 023rd day
- 132456789 = Check first 2 digits for year

**Older Systems:**
- 1953-1961: Ink stamped, 5-6 digits
- 1961-1969: Various formats
- 1970-1975: 6 digits with "Made in USA" stamp

**Custom Shop:** Different serialization, often includes "CS" prefix

##  Vintage Market Basics

### Value Factors

| Factor | Impact | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Brand** | High | Gibson, Fender, Martin top tier |
| **Model** | High | Iconic models command premiums |
| **Year** | Very High | Pre-CBS Fender, 50s Gibson = peak |
| **Condition** | Very High | Originality crucial for vintage |
| **Provenance** | Variable | Celebrity/studio history adds value |
| **Rarity** | Variable | Limited runs, unique specs |
| **Documentation** | Medium | Original paperwork helps |
| **Case** | Low-Medium | Original case adds 5-10% |

### Key Vintage Eras

**Fender:**
| Era | Years | Notes | Value |
|-----|-------|-------|-------|
| Pre-CBS | 1950-1965 | Leo Fender era | $$$$$ |
| CBS Early | 1965-1971 | Quality still good | $$$ |
| CBS Later | 1972-1984 | Decline period | $$ |
| Post-CBS | 1985-present | Employee buyout | $ - $$$ |

**Gibson:**
| Era | Years | Notes | Value |
|-----|-------|-------|-------|
| Golden Era | 1952-1960 | Original Les Paul run | $$$$$ |
| 60s | 1961-1969 | Pre-Norlin | $$$$ |
| Norlin | 1969-1986 | Corporate ownership | $$ |
| Post-Norlin | 1986-present | Quality resurgence | $ - $$$$ |

### Condition Grading

| Grade | Description | Value % |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Mint/NOS** | Unplayed, original everything | 100%+ |
| **Near Mint** | Minimal signs of use | 95-100% |
| **Excellent** | Light play wear | 85-95% |
| **Very Good** | Normal wear, fully functional | 70-85% |
| **Good** | Obvious wear, works fine | 50-70% |
| **Fair** | Heavy wear, may need work | 30-50% |
| **Poor** | Project/parts guitar | <30% |

### Originality Rules

For vintage guitars, modifications typically reduce value:
- **Refret**: Acceptable if done well, minor impact
- **Refinish**: Major impact (50%+ reduction)
- **Changed pickups**: Moderate impact (20-40%)
- **Changed hardware**: Minor to moderate
- **Changed tuners**: Minor impact
- **Electronics repair**: Acceptable
- **Headstock repair**: Major impact (30-60%)

##  Provenance Documentation

### What Adds Value

**Artist/Celebrity Connection:**
- Owned by famous musician (requires proof)
- Used on recordings or tours (photos/video)
- Signed or customized for artist

**Studio/Venue History:**
- Used in famous recording studio
- Part of historic live performance
- Featured in music video or film

**Documentation Types:**

| Type | Value Impact | Example |
|------|--------------|---------|
| **Photo proof** | High | Artist playing specific guitar |
| **Video footage** | Very High | Performance with visible serial |
| **Certificate** | Medium | Signed authentication |
| **Purchase receipt** | Medium | From artist or estate |
| **Backstage pass/credential** | Low-Medium | Supporting context |
| **Artist statement** | Very High | Written confirmation |

**Warning Signs of Fake Provenance:**
- No verifiable photos with serial visible
- Story passed through many hands
- "Believed to be" without documentation
- Price too good to be true
- Certificate from unknown source

##  Guitar Evaluation Criteria

When documenting/comparing guitars, consider these scoring areas:

| Category | What to Assess |
|----------|----------------|
| **Build Quality** | Materials, construction, fit & finish |
| **Playability** | Action, neck profile, fret quality |
| **Pickup Quality** | Tone clarity, output balance, noise |
| **Hardware Quality** | Tuner smoothness, bridge stability |
| **Versatility** | Range of usable tones |
| **Value** | Price vs. features/quality |
| **Rarity** | Production numbers, availability |
| **Collectibility** | Historical significance, demand |
