## üî¢ Serial Number Decoding

### Modern Gibson USA (1977-Present)

**8-Digit Format: YDDDYPPP**
- Y = Year digit (1st & 5th position)
- DDD = Day of year (001-365)
- PPP = Plant/sequence

**Examples:**
| Serial | Decode |
|--------|--------|
| 90234XXX | 1999, day 023 |
| 82765XXX | 1985, day 276 |
| 02345XXX | 2005, day 234 (post-2000 trickier) |

**Post-2000 Clarification:**
- First digit shows decade
- Sometimes YDDDYXXX where 5th digit also year
- Cross-reference with specs

### Older Systems

| Era | Format | Location |
|-----|--------|----------|
| 1953-1961 | 5-6 digit ink stamp | Headstock back |
| 1961-1969 | Various | Headstock back |
| 1970-1975 | 6 digits + "Made in USA" | Impressed |
| 1975-1977 | 8 digits (early system) | Varies |

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Identification Mistakes

### 1. Epiphone vs Gibson

**Always Check:**
- Headstock shape (Epiphone: different "open book")
- Logo style (Epiphone script vs Gibson)
- Truss rod cover (Gibson: "Bell" shape blank, Epi: usually has model name)
- Serial number prefix
- Binding quality
- Hardware weight

### 2. Real vs Fake Gibson

**Counterfeit Red Flags:**
- Serial doesn't match known formats
- Wrong font on headstock
- "Gibson" and "Les Paul" wrong spacing
- Poor binding
- Lightweight tuners
- Wrong pickup rings
- "Les Paul Model" on truss rod cover (real Gibson = blank)

### 3. Norlin vs Desirable

**Not All Bad:**
- Norlin era has good examples
- Check: weight, neck joint, playability
- Some are highly regarded (Artisan series)

### 4. Historic vs Standard

**Historic/Reissue tells:**
- Long neck tenon
- Historic on truss rod cover
- Hide glue construction
- Period-correct specs
- Higher price point
